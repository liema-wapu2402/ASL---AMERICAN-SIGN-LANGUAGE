WHAT IS ASL OR AMERICAN SIGN LANGUAGE?

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete and natural language used primarily by Deaf individuals in North America, as well as parts of Canada. Unlike spoken languages, ASL relies on visual-gestural communication, using handshapes, facial expressions, and body movements to convey meaning. It has its own unique grammar, syntax, and rules, distinct from English or any other spoken language. ASL is a rich and expressive language with a wide vocabulary, allowing users to communicate complex ideas and concepts. It is used in various settings, including education, social interactions, and professional environments.

It's important to note that ASL is not universal; different countries and regions have their own sign languages. For instance,British Sign Language (BSL) is distinct from ASL, despite both countries primarily speaking English.
